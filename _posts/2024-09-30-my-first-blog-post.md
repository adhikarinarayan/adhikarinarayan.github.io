## Countability and Natural Numbers
### Definition of Countability

A set is countable if its elements can be put in a one-to-one correspondence with the natural numbers or a subset of the natural numbers.

### Importance of Natural Numbers

- Natural numbers are our basic counting tool
- They provide a way to "order" elements, even in infinite sets
- Allow for theoretical enumeration of set elements

### Bijection

A one-to-one correspondence (bijection) ensures:
- Every element in the set is paired with a unique natural number (injection)
- Every natural number is paired with a unique element in the set (surjection)
